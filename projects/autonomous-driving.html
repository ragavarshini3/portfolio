Swarm Intelligence Autonomous Driving

Applying swarm intelligence and V2V node messaging to establish cooperative driving and collision avoidance parameters in simulation.

The Challenge

Modern autonomous vehicles operating individually rely solely on local sensors, creating blind spots and delayed reaction times in busy environments. To increase safety and optimize traffic flow, vehicles must communicate directly to make collaborative driving decisions.

The challenge lay in designing a distributed robotics simulation architecture where multiple vehicle agents could broadcast telemetry, dynamically adjust spacing in a convoy formation (platooning), and execute cooperative path-planning maneuvers using swarm intelligence principles.

The Solution

I designed and implemented a simulation prototype built on ROS2 middleware. By modeling individual vehicle agents as self-contained ROS2 nodes, I utilized publisher-subscriber communication channels to emulate V2V messaging logs. The coordination model leverages swarm intelligence: each node observes surrounding telemetry (speed, heading, distance vectors) and applies proportional control mechanics to coordinate velocity outputs. Tests were run in Gazebo Classic and CARLA environment maps to validate spacing robustness.

Key Challenges & Resolutions

  • Synchronizing Distributed Nodes: Message latency over ROS2 networks can cause vehicles to overshoot brakes. I resolved this by tuning QoS (Quality of Service) properties for transient-local parameters to ensure high-priority safety packets arrived reliably.
  • Convoy Collision Dampening: Hard braking at the front of a platoon typically propagates backwards, causing rear crashes (string instability). I implemented a custom damping algorithm that shares lead vehicle speed variables directly with all nodes, flattening decel curves.
  • Heterogeneous Simulator Sync: Bridging ROS2 nodes to CARLA coordinates required detailed mapping of message structures. I configured dedicated CARLA-ROS2 bridges to handle state conversions smoothly.
